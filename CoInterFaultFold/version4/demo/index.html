Interseismic Velocity Explorer

Interactive decomposition of the interseismic vertical velocity field for the NE Japan (Tohoku) subduction zone geometry.

The absolute interseismic velocity is:

\[v_{\text{inter}}^{\,\text{abs}} = (1-\alpha)\,w(z)\,v_{\text{struct}} - v_{\text{co}}\]

where \(\alpha\) partitions the vertical budget between hanging-wall uplift and footwall subsidence, controlled by slab buoyancy.

\(v_{\text{struct}}(x) = \sin\theta(\xi^*(x))\) is the Suppe kinematic velocity mapped through the axial surface projection.

\(w(z)\) is the transmission coefficient, representing the transition from rigid Suppe kinematics (valid in brittle crust) to distributed ductile flow (no discrete uplift). Tapers via arctan at the onset depth.

\(v_{\text{co}}\) is the elastic back-slip from the locked zone, computed via edge dislocation Green's functions (Savage 1983).

Fault: \(z = -(0.12\,x + 0.0007\,x^2)\) km
Dip: 6.8° to 34.6° over 300 km
\(V_p\): 83 mm/yr (Pacific Plate)

\(\alpha=1\): Savage BSM (zero baseline).
\(\alpha=0\): Himalayan limit (full structural uplift).
\(w=1\): Suppe fully valid.
\(w\to 0\): deep/hot, no discrete uplift.

Steady-state: (1-α)·w·vstruct
Locked zone: vco
