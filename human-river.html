Human-River Geomorphic Coupling

by Brayden Noh and Omar Wani

Meandering rivers attract civilization — providing food, water, and transportation — but also destroy it when they shift course. Human-River couples these two forces into a single dynamical system on a cellular automaton.

Each cell on a 2D grid is either river, settlement, or empty floodplain. Population is conserved: if the river drowns a settlement, it must relocate. Each cell's utility is

\[ U(\mathbf{x}, t) \;=\; -\,w_d \, D(\mathbf{x}, t) \;+\; w_c \, C(\mathbf{x}, t) \]

where \(D\) is the distance to the nearest river cell, \(C\) is the number of occupied Moore neighbors, and \(w_d, w_c\) are tunable coupling weights. River cells have \(U = -\infty\).

Two rules drive the dynamics each timestep:

  1. Bulldozer. Settlements drowned by the migrating river are erased and regrown at the highest-utility perimeter cell.
  2. Crawl. The worst settlement swaps with the best empty perimeter cell, but only if the utility gain exceeds a friction threshold \(\mu\).
