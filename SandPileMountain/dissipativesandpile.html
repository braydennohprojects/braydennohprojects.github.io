Dissipative sandpile. At each step, one grain is dropped randomly on an active cell, and at each iteration every cell (height h) compares itself with its D8 neighborhood and picks out the lowest value among the 8 neighbors, then topples whenever the relative drop from h to that lowest value reaches SLOPE — when that happens, an amount defined by ERODE is subtracted from h and those blocks simply leave the grid (no redistribution to any neighbor), and the check repeats on the affected cells until nothing is unstable anymore.
